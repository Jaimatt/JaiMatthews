Game of the Year

Jai Matthews ⋅ Ongoing

You’ve found my game list! I don’t tend to advertise that this is a thing, but I’ve been doing this for a few years sort of as an exercise in reflection or perhaps just writing. In any case, I thought I’d post it here on my website.

So how does it work? Well, every year, once a year, I choose one game I played through the year that I enjoyed the most. They’re not necessarily the most advanced game, not necessarily the most popular , they don’t even have to have been released during the year in question! Rather, the chosen game is merely the one that I loved the most, that had the greatest effect on me, or that had been the most important to me throughout my year.

Let’s get started!

2017

Super Mario Odyssey

Super Mario Odyssey was actually the reason I bought a Switch in the first place.

Ever the Mario fan, I was thrilled to experience a sprawling world full of unique and imaginative landscapes through which I could “Wah”, “Hoo”, and “Wahoo” to my heart's content. 

The property of the game that had me the most excited was the expertly refined quality of Mario’s moveset. Super Mario Odyssey features a host of satisfying jumps & tricks Mario can perform. His movement strikes a perfect balance of being easy to learn, but hard to master. And it filled me with an insatiable desire to get to the next ledge, reach the next power moon, and complete the next secret area. I always felt rewarded by my progress. Super Mario Odyssey is a standout game for the Switch and I will be playing it for years to come!

2018

Minecraft

Minecraft needs no introduction. And, in fact, I was already extremely familiar with the game prior to the dawn of 2018. 

Minecraft’s innate freedom gives way to ample reasons to come back to the game time & time again. The freeing feeling this sandbox survival game gives is one that is captured by shockingly few games. I chose Minecraft as my pick for 2018 because this is the year where I felt I properly experienced the immense breadth of Minecraft’s diverse offerings. The year saw me play through some exciting survival worlds with friends, build (to my mind at least) elegant structures, enthusiastically learn the new 1.13 command structure and design all my favourite minigames. It was amazing to experience so many of the things that makes Minecraft as popular as it is.

2019

The Legend of Zelda - Breath of the Wild

Two years. It took me two years to finally get around to playing what was to become one of my favourite video games of all time. Breath of the Wild initially seemed outside of my comfort zone for video games. An adventure RPG? A moving & voice-acted narrative? A daunting sense of complete freedom? Clearly outside of the safety of the marios & pokemons a younger me would stubbornly cling to. But when I eventually convinced myself to take the risk, I found the richest, most moving, and most empowering game I’ve played. Breath of the Wild masterfully blends challenge, intrigue and charm into a game startlingly close to perfection. Breath of the Wild was the perfect game to break me out of the box I’d trapped myself in and thus stands as not just a game, but also a genuinely important experience for me.

2020

Ori & the Blind Forest

Prompted by the release of Will of the Wisps, I decided to play the original Ori game; Ori & the Blind Forest. What I was greeted with was a well-designed Metroidvania with an astonishingly beautiful presentation unlike anything I’d ever played. From the moment I booted up the game, I was overwhelmed by the beautiful vista of the Ginso tree set to its iconic choir-backed soundtrack. The emotional prologue further helped to set the tone of this game & all throughout the game I was impressed by the immaculate beauty Moon Studios put into the world of Nibel. Additionally, the gameplay was very fun! I particularly enjoyed using the innovative “Bash” ability & the exciting, albeit stressful, escape sequences after each of the game's dungeons. Despite being in a genre I don’t usually enjoy, Ori captured my heart & is a true work of art.

2021

Metroid Dread

Metroid Dread is great!

I should probably write more about it but... I’m worried the E.M.M.I. might hear me! Shhh!

Seriously though play it. It’s terrifying, but awesome.

2022

Inside

And we’re back to another Indie game! Metroid Dread threw us off last year, but we thankfully return to our rightful place. Truthfully, in a year that saw both Super Mario Bros. Wonder AND Pikmin 4, it surprised even me that the modest puzzle platformer Inside made it to the top of my list.

Inside is awesome. To be honest, writing this is quite hard. And for two reasons. The first is that Inside is not a particularly innovative nor impressive game. I actually find it difficult to pinpoint just where its excellence stems from. Perhaps, it’s the perfectly curated atmosphere of the game? Or even some of the grotesque imagery, which left the game seared into my brain (in the best way possible!). Like it did to me, it’s a game that will leave you thinking about it for a long time to come.

The second reason is that I don’t want to spoil it! Almost anything I have to say might ruin one element of it. Whenever I recommend it to someone, I just tell them to stop listening to me and go play it! You certainly will not regret it!

2023

Celeste

Before I start, I should probably acknowledge the Tears of the Kingdom in the room. I’ll confess I did not particularly enjoy Tears of the Kingdom. And, in any case, before I could get to it, another game came to me. One I was not prepared to love the way I did.

Celeste had been on my radar for a very long time. Probably since its release. For some reason I thought that the game was a Metroidvania? Not being a huge fan of the genre, I filed the game under the “will get around to eventually” category. When, in April of this year, I discovered that it was, in fact, a pure platformer , I practically bought it on the spot! And I am so glad I did. In my mind Celeste is not a particularly innovative concept, but rather excels at the fact that every facet of its creation is carried out to an almost impossibly high standard. The moveset? The best I’ve ever played. The level design? Every screen is fun, every death is fair. The artwork? It doesn’t cease to blow me away despite the limitations of its medium. The Music? I am always in absolute awe of Lena Raine’s talent. And the story? Well, this is what truly fueled my interest in Celeste. A moving tale of overcoming anxiety & depression, told through a fantasy setting & a genius central metaphor. In addition to all this, it was really fascinating to think about Madeline’s transformative journey to be one of gender identity - a theme oft-neglect in the gaming industry! All in all, Celeste was a breathtaking experience that I would recommend to anyone interested in gaming, platforming, or just great stories.

You will cry though. It’s hard. So, so fucking hard.

2024

Celeste

Okay, hear me out!

I know this was my pick for last year. So, instead of reviewing the game as usual, allow me to persuade you as to why my blatant repetition is justified. First of all, I really didn’t play many games this year. I got back into Minecraft a bit, ultimately deciding I was better off without it. And I played The Legend of Zelda: Echoes of Wisdom, even if I was not a massive fan (though by no means was it a bad game!).

But, for the most part, in the moments I did find myself gaming, it was Celeste. There was still plenty I hadn’t done. C-Sides, Golden Strawberries, even Farewell (because that bloody comb room could fuck off!!!). In many ways it’s become something of a comfort game. Something I could always go back to. Maybe I could try to beat my time on 2A? Get 6C golden? Try again for MoonBerry? If I may permit myself a moment of shameless bragging, I’ve become quite good at the game! As of writing, I have 100% as well as 12 golden strawberries (that’s right, bitch. get on my level).

To be honest, there’s not too much else to say given I’ve just chosen the same game again. I guess it was just that good. I’ll be back again in 2025, this time with a different game, I promise!
